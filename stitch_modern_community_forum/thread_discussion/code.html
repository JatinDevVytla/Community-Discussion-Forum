The Silent Revolution of Brutalist Minimalism in Digital Interfaces

We are witnessing a shift where high-contrast typography and raw geometric shapes are reclaiming the space once dominated by soft gradients and complex shadows. This isn't just an aesthetic choice; it's a functional push toward cognitive clarity.

In this thread, I want to explore how "intentional friction" can actually lead to better user retention. When we stop making everything "invisible," the user starts to engage with the architecture of the information itself.
